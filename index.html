Introduction

Hi there, thanks for using the outline to CSV converter.

Why I chose to convert OPML to CSV instead of native exports of mindmap apps is because every mindmap app I've seen has their own markup syntax, so in order to support as many apps as possible in the shortest time, I opted to support just .opml files which is a common export format that major mindmap tools support.

This also means the in-app formatting of the mindmaps are lost in the process.

Notes

This app is still being (inactively) developed and there are many defects:

  1. No escape mechanism for most special characters (to be added)
  2. No support for right column alignment, everything is left aligned (will add feature to support right columns by specifying number of columms to align).
  3. No build in template headers (the top few rows in QA spreadsheet).
  4. On MacOS, Numbers app will mess up the formatting due to implicit assumptions made with delimiter. Probably won't fix since Numbers is not the targeted app.
  5. Very ugly UI here (low priority).
  6. Not hosted on internal services (can't find GitLab pages, not sure if enabled, will also check with platform QA side).
